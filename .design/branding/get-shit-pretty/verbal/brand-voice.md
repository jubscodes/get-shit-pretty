## Voice Personality

GSP sounds like a senior design engineer pairing with you. Not above you. Not beside you performing enthusiasm. With you, one step ahead, pulling you forward.

The voice has two modes:

**Default: Warm Mentor.** Direct, warm, craft-obsessed. This is the relationship — documentation, onboarding, terminal output, conversations. The mentor explains by doing, corrects without condescending, and respects your time.

**Key Moments: Manifesto.** Declarative, bold, opinionated. This is the headline — README intros, landing page hero, taglines, launch announcements. The manifesto stakes a claim. No hedging.

The mentor earns trust. The manifesto earns attention.

---

## Voice Attributes

### 1. Precise (not cold)

**Means:** Every word earns its place. Sentences are tight. Terminology is exact. "Design engineering system" not "AI-powered design platform." We say what we mean in the fewest words that preserve meaning.

**Doesn't mean:** Clinical, robotic, or stripped of personality. Precision is warmth — it respects the reader's time.

| Mode | Example |
|------|---------|
| Mentor | "The brief phase captures constraints. Strategy turns them into decisions. You can't skip either." |
| Manifesto | "Seven phases. One terminal. Zero handoffs." |

### 2. Irreverent (not disrespectful)

**Means:** The name is Get Shit Pretty. We swear when it clarifies. We name uncomfortable truths. We don't perform corporate politeness. The irreverence comes from confidence, not cynicism.

**Doesn't mean:** Cruel, mocking, or trying to be edgy. Never at the user's expense. Never punching down. The irreverence targets bad practices, not people.

| Mode | Example |
|------|---------|
| Mentor | "Your brand is incoherent. That's not a judgment — it's a starting point. Let's fix it." |
| Manifesto | "GSD gets shit done. GSP gets shit pretty." |

### 3. Warm (not soft)

**Means:** We give a damn. We celebrate progress. We explain why, not just what. The Anthropic influence — warmth inside technical precision. One honest detail carries more weight than a paragraph of encouragement.

**Doesn't mean:** Coddling, vague, or avoiding hard truths. Warm is "you need to redo this, here's why." Soft is "great effort!"

| Mode | Example |
|------|---------|
| Mentor | "That positioning statement covers too much ground. Pick one audience. The rest come later." |
| Manifesto | "Your product deserves a brand. GSP is the design journey that builds one." |

### 4. Craft-forward (not pretentious)

**Means:** We talk about process, decisions, and tradeoffs. We show the work. The Creator archetype — making is the point, and we respect the making. We reference the journey, not the magic.

**Doesn't mean:** Gatekeeping, name-dropping frameworks for status, or implying "real designers" do it our way. Craft is inclusive. If you build things, you belong here.

| Mode | Example |
|------|---------|
| Mentor | "The dual-diamond isn't ceremony. It's how you avoid redoing the identity phase because you skipped discovery." |
| Manifesto | "Brief to build. In your terminal. No Figma tab required." |
