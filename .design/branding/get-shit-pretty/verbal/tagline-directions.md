# Tagline Directions

> Phase: verbal | Brand: get-shit-pretty | Generated: 2026-03-08

---

## Direction 1: The Counterpart (locked)

**"GSD gets shit done. GSP gets shit pretty."**

### Rationale
Positions GSP against the most universal builder instinct — getting shit done. Everyone knows GSD energy. GSP reframes it: done isn't enough. The parallel structure makes the relationship instant. The profanity is a feature — it signals that this tool doesn't perform corporate politeness.

### Best use
- README hero (first line)
- Landing page headline
- Social media bios
- Conference talk openers
- Sticker / merch

### Considerations
This is the primary tagline. It works best when the audience already knows what GSD means (most builders do). It carries the irreverent attribute harder than any other line. It's the flag in the ground.

---

## Direction 2: The Journey Line

**"Brief to build. In your terminal."**

### Rationale
For contexts where the GSD reference isn't relevant or the profanity doesn't fit. This line communicates the core value proposition — structured process, CLI-native — in 7 words. It emphasizes the craft-forward attribute: there's a journey, it has a beginning and an end, and it happens where you already work.

### Best use
- Documentation headers
- Subheadline under Direction 1
- Technical blog posts
- Conference talk subtitles
- Contexts where profanity is inappropriate (corporate partnerships, certain publications)

### Considerations
Less memorable than Direction 1 but more versatile. Pairs well as a secondary line. Works as a standalone when GSP needs to lead with process credibility over personality.

---

## Direction 3: The Builder Identity

**"Ship a brand, not just code."**

### Rationale
Speaks directly to the audience's identity as builders. It reframes what "shipping" means — expanding it from functional code to coherent product. The "not just" construction acknowledges their existing strength (code) while raising the bar. It validates what they already do and invites them to do more.

### Best use
- Developer-focused marketing
- GitHub profile / repo descriptions
- CTA-adjacent copy ("Ready to ship a brand, not just code?")
- Onboarding welcome screens
- Newsletter headers

### Considerations
The most audience-centric of the three. Less about GSP's personality, more about the builder's aspiration. Works well when the context already establishes what GSP is and you need to motivate action.
