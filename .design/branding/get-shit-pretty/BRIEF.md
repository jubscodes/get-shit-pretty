# Brand Brief

## Brand
- **Name:** get-shit-pretty
- **Date:** 2026-03-08

## Company
- **Company name:** Get Shit Pretty (GSP)
- **Industry:** Developer tools / Design engineering
- **Founded:** 2025
- **Size:** Solo creator (jubscodes)
- **Existing brand?** No — starting from zero

## Brand Mode
- **Mode:** new
- **Reason:** First brand identity for an established open-source CLI tool

## Mission & Vision
- **Mission:** Guide builders from zero to one to lasting product — not just delivery, but coherent craft that holds up as you ship and evolve.
- **Vision:** Make design engineering the default discipline for CLI builders. Every product ships with brand coherence, not just code quality.
- **Values:** Craft, coherence, curiosity, builder empowerment, design as a journey (not a deliverable)

## Audience
- **Primary (Developers):** Developers who gain product thinking through GSP. They know how to build but ship code, not product. GSP gives them design agents that turn "I need a landing page" into a coherent brand-to-build journey. They stop delivering features and start crafting products.
- **Primary (Designers):** Designers who gain a development tool that speaks their language. They know craft but are disconnected from this new era of conversational creation. GSP connects their design thinking to the terminal, where products are now built. They stop handing off and start building.
- **Convergence:** Both audiences meet at design engineering — the rising discipline between developer and designer. GSP is the bridge. The shared identity is "builders" — people who craft, who delve into the depths of problems to reorganize systems and bring products to being.
- **Demographics:** Technical and creative professionals using AI coding tools (Claude Code, OpenCode, Gemini CLI, Codex). Solo creators, startup teams, open-source contributors. Comfortable in the terminal or ready to be.
- **Psychographics:** Craft-obsessed. Care about the whole product, not just their lane. Value depth over speed (but appreciate both). Love the feeling of shipping something that looks as good as it works. Identify as builders more than their job title.

## Personality
- **Brand personality:** CLI-first, curious, creative. A design mentor who lives in the terminal. Pushes you to improve with love for the craft — never talks down, always levels you up.
- **Tone:** Confident, irreverent, direct. Swears at the desk but ships pixel-perfect. No BS, no jargon walls. Speaks developer, thinks designer.
- **How should the brand feel?** Like a talented friend who happens to be a design engineer — someone who geeks out about spacing and color but also understands your codebase. Approachable expertise.

## Competitive Landscape
- **Direct competitors:** No direct competitor — GSP occupies a new niche at the intersection of AI-assisted development and structured design process. Adjacent tools: shadcn/ui (component library), Figma (design tool), Storybook (component docs).
- **What sets you apart?** GSP is a journey, not a tool. It guides through the full brand-to-build process inside the terminal. Others give you pieces; GSP gives you the system and walks you through it.
- **Brands admired:** Teenage Engineering (a child's unbounded imagination with an old craftsman's precision — limitation as liberation, design as interface, playfulness as professionalism), Vercel (monochrome confidence, type system as identity, proof a dev tool can be this refined), Anthropic (warmth meets technical craft, one symbolic detail carries the story, dual audiences united by warm brand), Figma (expanding audience without diluting, community as growth, playful but serious), Omarchy (terminal-native beauty as philosophy — "aesthetics are the point," omakase curation over infinite choice, ASCII art as genuine brand identity, one theme file propagates system-wide coherence, proof that developers want beautiful terminals)
- **Positioning note:** Linear defined a category with dark mode craft but has become overused as a brand template. GSP should be avant-garde — break past the Linear aesthetic, not replicate it.

## Inspiration
- **Styles liked:** Opinionated minimalism with personality. CLI aesthetic elevated to design quality. The beauty of a well-formatted terminal output. Dark mode native. Monospace as a design choice, not a limitation.
- **Styles to avoid:** Corporate, generic SaaS, agency-polished, cutesy. Anything that feels like "we hired a branding agency." Should feel built by someone who ships.
- **Existing assets:** None — starting from zero

## Constraints
- **Timeline:** No hard deadline — quality over speed
- **Budget:** Open source / bootstrapped — no paid asset licensing
- **Must-haves:** Works in terminal context (ASCII-friendly logo mark), dark mode native, strong in monochrome (color is additive, not required)
- **Non-negotiables:** The name stays. The irreverence stays. Must feel CLI-native.

## Goals
- **Business goal:** Establish GSP as the design engineering system for CLI builders. Grow adoption across Claude Code, OpenCode, Gemini, and Codex ecosystems.
- **Brand goal:** Create a recognizable identity that signals craft, confidence, and builder culture. The brand itself should be proof that GSP works.
- **Success metrics:** Brand recognition in the AI coding tool community. The identity becomes a reference for "dev tool that cares about design." Contributors and users feel proud to use it.

## Founder Ecosystem

GSP exists within a coherent founder ecosystem. Three brands, same craft DNA, distinct identities:

| Brand | Role | Visual DNA | Personality |
|-------|------|-----------|-------------|
| **jubs.studio** | Personal brand / solo design engineering studio | Cyberpunk dark. Neon green (#00FF88) + burnt orange. Space Grotesk + JetBrains Mono. CRT scanlines, ASCII borders. | "Opinion is a feature. Bland is a choice." |
| **cyphercn** | Terminal component library (shadcn-like) | Retro-terminal aesthetic. Accessible components with retro design. | The building blocks — terminal-native components |
| **GSP** | Design engineering system | Own identity (TBD) — distinct from jubs.studio and cyphercn but same family | The journey — precise, irreverent, unexpected |

Like Guillermo Rauch → Vercel → Next.js: distinct brands, shared craft DNA. GSP should feel like a sibling, not a clone. Terminal-native precision carries through, but GSP owns its own color, energy, and voice.

**Ecosystem connections:**
- GSP can use cyphercn components in its build outputs
- jubs.studio showcases both as portfolio pieces (founder-market fit)
- Shared values: terminal-native, opinionated, craft-forward, dark-first
- **Key proof point:** jubs.studio was designed using GSP (v0.1/0.2). The tool's first shipped product is the founder's own brand. GSP designed the designer.

## Notes
- GSP is meta — it's a design tool designing itself. The brand should be living proof of the system's quality.
- Key insight from brief: AI makes building fast, so brand coherence becomes the real challenge. GSP is the through-line that keeps products being themselves as they evolve.
- Dual-audience strategy: developers gain product agents, designers gain a dev tool that speaks their language. Both converge at design engineering.
- "Builders" is the shared identity but not the targeting — you find them as developers and designers first, then unite them.
- The codebase is a CLI/tooling package (Node.js, npm) — no UI framework. Any visual identity work (website, docs) would be a separate design project.
