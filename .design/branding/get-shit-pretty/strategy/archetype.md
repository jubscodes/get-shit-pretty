# Archetype

> Phase: strategy | Brand: get-shit-pretty | Generated: 2026-03-08

---

## Primary: The Creator (70%)

**Core desire:** Innovation, self-expression, creating something enduring.
**Brand promise:** If it can be imagined, it can be created.

GSP exists so that builders can make something that lasts. Not ship fast, not iterate to infinity -- create something enduring. A brand system that holds up at v1, v2, v10. The Creator archetype is the engine of GSP's identity: every feature, every pipeline phase, every design token exists to serve the act of creation.

### Why Creator fits GSP

1. **Prism alignment: Physique** -- The avant-garde visual language (monospace, true black, thick borders, ASCII marks, burnt orange heat) is a Creator's aesthetic: craft-forward, experimental, designed to inspire making.
2. **Prism alignment: Culture** -- "Craft over speed," "constraints as creative fuel," "design as journey" are pure Creator values. The Creator doesn't rush; the Creator refines.
3. **Prism alignment: Self-Image** -- "I ship craft, not just code" is the Creator's internal monologue. The user becomes a maker through GSP.
4. **Prism alignment: Personality** -- Precise and unexpected map directly to the Creator's perfectionism and inventiveness.

### Creator exemplar: Teenage Engineering

TE is the Creator archetype made physical. A synthesizer company that designs objects so beautiful they redefine what "music hardware" looks like. They treat constraints (pocket-sized, limited buttons, modular form factors) as the creative canvas. GSP takes the same approach: the terminal is not a limitation, it's the creative medium. TE's principle -- "a child's unbounded imagination with an old craftsman's precision" -- is GSP's operating philosophy.

### Creator community: Figma's model

The Creator archetype can become solitary and self-indulgent. Figma solved this by making creation collaborative -- the best design systems are community-built, forked, evolved. GSP inherits this: open source, ecosystem orchestration, shared brand systems. The Creator builds, but the community multiplies.

### Creator curation: Omarchy's model

Omarchy (DHH / 37signals) embodies the Creator as curator -- "omakase" means trusting the chef's selection. DHH's principle that "aesthetics are the point" validates GSP's core bet: developers want beautiful tools. Omarchy's theme system (one `colors.toml` propagates system-wide coherence) is a direct parallel to GSP's token system (one `tokens.json` propagates brand coherence). Both prove that structured curation beats infinite customization. Omarchy beautifies the environment; GSP beautifies the output.

## Secondary: The Magician (30%)

**Core desire:** Transformation, making dreams real.
**Brand promise:** It can happen.

The Magician is the transformation engine. GSP doesn't just help you create -- it transforms what you thought was possible. A developer who never touched brand strategy now has a coherent positioning statement. A designer who never opened a terminal now ships components. The before/after gap is where the Magician lives.

### Why Magician as secondary

1. **Prism alignment: Relationship** -- The mentor who transforms you. Not just teaches -- transforms. You walk in as a developer, you walk out as a design engineer.
2. **Prism alignment: Personality** -- "Unexpected" is the Magician's trait. The output exceeds expectations. "Wait, it did THAT?" is transformation language.
3. **Prism alignment: Reflection** -- The builder who ships products that look as good as they work. That transformation from "just works" to "works AND looks incredible" is Magician territory.

### Magician exemplar: The GSP pipeline itself

The dual-diamond pipeline is literally a transformation engine. Brief goes in, brand system comes out. Vague idea goes in, design-engineered product comes out. The structured journey (discover, strategy, verbal, identity, system) is the Magician's ritual -- each phase transforms the input into something the user didn't know they could produce.

## Shadow Traits to Manage

### Creator shadows

| Shadow | Risk for GSP | Mitigation |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| **Perfectionism paralysis** | Users get stuck refining instead of shipping. Pipeline phases never feel "done." | Pipeline has explicit phase gates. "Good enough to move forward" is built into the methodology. Ship, then iterate. |
| **Self-indulgence** | Brand output becomes impressive to other designers but irrelevant to the audience. Navel-gazing. | Every phase ties back to the brief. Audience validation is baked into the process. The craft serves the builder, not the brand strategist's ego. |
| **Impracticality** | Beautiful brand system that can't be implemented. Tokens that don't map to real components. | GSP's pipeline ends in BUILD, not DESIGN. Everything must compile, render, and ship. Aesthetic serves function. |

### Magician shadows

| Shadow | Risk for GSP | Mitigation |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| **Over-promising** | "Turn any developer into a design engineer" sets expectations that crash on contact with reality. | Messaging emphasizes the journey, not the instant result. GSP is a guide, not a magic wand. Transformation takes work. |
| **Disconnection from reality** | AI-generated brand systems that feel synthetic, detached from the builder's actual context. | The brief phase is mandatory. User input drives everything. GSP doesn't generate in a vacuum -- it generates from YOUR context, YOUR audience, YOUR product. |
| **Manipulation** | Making the transformation feel effortless when it isn't, creating dependency on the tool. | Open-source ethos. GSP teaches as it builds. Users understand what they're shipping. The goal is builder empowerment, not tool dependency. |

## Communication Style

### Voice characteristics

- **Lead with the work, not the promise.** Show the output. Let the transformation speak for itself. Creator shows, Magician explains.
- **Precise language.** No filler words, no marketing bloat. Every sentence earns its place. Vercel's copy density is the benchmark.
- **Irreverent tone.** The subject matter is serious (brand strategy, design systems). The delivery is not. Swearing is fine when it serves clarity. Jargon is fine when the audience speaks it. Pretension is never fine.
- **Transformational framing.** Before/after. Input/output. What you had / what you have now. The gap is the story.
- **Craft-forward.** Talk about spacing, tokens, type scales, color ratios. The details ARE the story. Anthropic's approach: one symbolic detail carries the whole narrative.

### What GSP sounds like

- "Your brand is incoherent. Let's fix that."
- "Brief to build. In your terminal. No Figma tab required."
- "GSD gets shit done. GSP gets shit pretty."
- "You shipped code. Now ship a brand."
- "The pipeline has seven phases because your brand deserves more than a color picker."

### What GSP never sounds like

- "Unlock your creative potential with our AI-powered solution."
- "We're passionate about helping you succeed."
- "Our cutting-edge platform leverages..."
- "Join thousands of happy customers."
- Anything that could appear on a competitor's landing page without changes.

## Visual Tendencies (Creator + Magician)

The archetype blend drives specific visual choices:

- **Creator:** Craft-forward layouts. Visible structure. The grid is part of the design, not hidden behind it. Monospace as identity. Thick borders. Engineering exposed.
- **Magician:** Transformation moments. Before/after reveals. The burnt orange against black is the Magician's reveal -- heat emerging from void, the citric surprise.
- **Combined:** Structured experimental. The layout is rigorous (Creator) but the details surprise (Magician). A swiss grid that uses ASCII art. A type system that puts JetBrains Mono where you'd expect a display serif. Neubrutalism that feels warm instead of aggressive.
