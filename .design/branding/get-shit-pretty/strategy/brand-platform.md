# Brand Platform

> Phase: strategy | Brand: get-shit-pretty | Generated: 2026-03-08

---

## Purpose

**Make design engineering the default discipline for CLI builders.**

Not "democratize design." Not "empower creators." A specific, measurable ambition: every builder who works in a terminal should have access to a structured design journey. The discipline of design engineering -- where brand strategy, visual systems, and code generation are one pipeline -- should be as standard as linting or testing.

---

## Vision

**Every product ships with brand coherence, not just code quality.**

The future GSP is building toward: a world where "does it work?" and "does it hold together as a brand?" are the same question. Where CI/CD includes brand consistency checks. Where a solo developer's side project has the same brand coherence as a funded startup's v1. Where "design engineer" is not a rare hybrid title but the default builder identity.

---

## Mission

**Guide builders from zero to one to lasting product -- not just delivery, but coherent craft that holds up as you ship and evolve.**

GSP's job is the journey. Not generating a component. Not producing a color palette. The full arc: from "I have an idea" to "I have a brand system that generates consistent, shippable output." And then staying with the builder as the product evolves -- because brands are living systems, not deliverables.

---

## Values

Five values. Each is behavioral -- an action, not an aspiration. Each has a "this means we..." and a "this means we don't..." to prevent the value from becoming a poster on a wall.

### 1. Craft over speed

**This means we:** Take seven phases to get from brief to build. Require a brief before generation. Challenge weak positioning. Validate before we ship. Build phase gates that prevent incoherent output.

**This means we don't:** Optimize for "fastest time to output." Skip phases. Let users bypass the brief. Celebrate speed at the cost of coherence. Ship a v0-style instant generator.

### 2. Coherence over novelty

**This means we:** Build brand systems, not individual assets. Ensure every token, component, and message traces back to the strategy. Prefer the boring-but-consistent decision over the exciting-but-fragmenting one.

**This means we don't:** Chase trends. Add features that break the system's consistency. Let agents generate output that contradicts the brand prism. Prioritize "wow" over "holds together."

### 3. Constraints as creative fuel

**This means we:** Treat the terminal as a design medium, not a limitation. Use monospace typography as identity, not compromise. Build within CLI constraints and find the aesthetic in those constraints. Ship ASCII art because it works in the medium.

**This means we don't:** Apologize for being CLI-native. Build a GUI "because real design tools have GUIs." Treat constraints as problems to solve rather than canvases to create on. Envy browser-based tools.

### 4. Builder empowerment

**This means we:** Open-source everything. Teach as we build. Explain the strategy behind the generation. Let builders own, fork, and evolve their output. Orchestrate the ecosystem instead of replacing it.

**This means we don't:** Create dependency on GSP. Generate black-box output that builders don't understand. Lock builders into proprietary formats. Replace Figma, Storybook, or component libraries -- we wire them together.

### 5. Design as journey

**This means we:** Structure the pipeline as phases with transformation between each. Make the process visible and valuable, not just the output. Treat iteration as a feature. Support brand evolution over time.

**This means we don't:** Promise instant results. Treat brand as a one-time deliverable. Skip the "boring" phases (brief, discovery, strategy) to get to the "fun" ones (identity, build). Pretend transformation happens without effort.

## Promise

**Ship a brand, not just code.**

What GSP guarantees to every builder who completes the pipeline: you will ship a coherent brand system -- positioning, voice, visual identity, design tokens, components -- not just functional code. Your product will hold together. Your brand will last.

## Tagline

**GSD gets shit done. GSP gets shit pretty.**

This tagline works because:
- It's irreverent -- the personality in seven words.
- It positions against the culture (GSD / "get shit done" is the developer's mantra) and extends it.
- It's memorable, repeatable, and works as a rallying cry.
- It implies: doing is necessary but insufficient. Pretty is the next level. Craft is the differentiator.

## Brand Essence (2 words)

**Enduring craft.**

Everything GSP does -- the pipeline, the agents, the methodology -- serves this: making things that are crafted, and making things that last. Enduring (the Creator's desire to make something permanent) + craft (the Magician's transformation of raw material into something beautiful).
