## WHY

**We believe every product deserves a brand that lasts -- not just code that works.**

The vibe coding era ships fast and breaks brands. Developers generate interfaces in seconds but can't answer "what does this product stand for?" Designers create beautiful mockups that shatter on implementation. The gap between "it runs" and "it holds together" is where products die slowly -- not from bugs, but from incoherence.

GSP exists because craft matters. Because the difference between a side project and a product is a brand. Because the builder who ships something coherent -- positioning, voice, visual system, components -- ships something that lasts. And lasting is the point.

This is not purpose-washing. This is not "making the world better through design." This is a specific, practical belief: if you're going to build something, build it with a brand. The brand is not decoration. The brand is the product's skeleton.

### Why this Why is ownable

- Figma believes in collaborative design, not brand coherence for builders.
- Vercel believes in shipping fast, not the brand journey.
- shadcn believes in accessible components, not strategic brand systems.
- No tool in the ecosystem says: "Your product needs a brand. Here's the structured journey to get one. In your terminal."

---

## HOW

**A structured design journey inside your terminal -- from brief to brand to build, guided by AI agents that think design.**

The dual-diamond pipeline is the How. It's not a feature; it's a methodology:

1. **It's structured.** Seven phases, not a blank canvas. Brief before research. Strategy before identity. Build after critique. The structure prevents the shortcuts that create incoherent brands.

2. **It's a journey.** Each phase transforms the input. Discovery transforms ignorance into insight. Strategy transforms insight into positioning. Identity transforms positioning into a visual system. Build transforms a system into shippable components. The journey is the product.

3. **It's in your terminal.** Not a browser tab. Not a SaaS dashboard. Not a Figma plugin. The terminal is where builders live. GSP meets them there. The constraint (CLI) is the creative advantage (no context-switching, no GUI overhead, direct integration with the codebase).

4. **AI agents that think design.** Not general-purpose chatbots with a design prompt. Specialized agents for brand strategy, verbal identity, visual systems, component architecture. Each agent has a methodology, references, and quality standards. The agents are opinionated because good design mentors are opinionated.

### How this How differentiates

- v0/shadcn: Generate components. No journey. No brand. No structure.
- Figma Make: Canvas to code. GUI-first. No terminal. No brand strategy.
- Claude Code raw: Ad-hoc generation. No pipeline. No design methodology. Stateless.
- GSP: Structured journey. Terminal-native. Brand-first. Stateful pipeline with phase gates.

## WHAT

**GSP is a design engineering system for CLI builders.**

Concrete product facts:

- **Dual-diamond pipeline** -- Branding diamond (discover, strategy, verbal, identity, system) + Project diamond (brief, research, design, critique, build, review). Optional launch phase.
- **Multi-runtime** -- Works with Claude Code, OpenCode, Gemini CLI, Codex. Not locked to one AI provider.
- **Open source** -- MIT licensed. Fork it, extend it, make it yours.
- **Ecosystem orchestrator** -- Connects with Figma, Storybook, component libraries (including cyphercn). Generates design tokens, component specs, and test-ready code. Doesn't replace your tools; wires them together.
- **Chunked output** -- Every phase produces self-contained, cross-referenced markdown chunks. No monoliths. Composable by design.
- **Agent specialization** -- Brand strategist, verbal identity writer, visual identity designer, design engineer agents. Each with domain expertise, not generic generation.

### What makes the What credible

The product architecture proves the How, which proves the Why. The pipeline has phases because brand coherence requires structure. The agents are specialized because design engineering requires domain expertise. The output is chunked because real design systems are modular. Every product decision traces back to the belief that products deserve brands that last.

And the proof: jubs.studio was designed by GSP. The tool's own creator used it to build their brand. The designer was designed by the design system.
