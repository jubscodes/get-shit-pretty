### 1. Physique

The tangible, visible brand. What you see before you read a word.

- **True black base** (#000000) -- not charcoal, not "dark mode gray." Actual black. The terminal is the canvas, not a compromise.
- **Burnt orange accent** (#FF6B35) -- citric heat against void. Not neon, not pastel. Classy and unexpected -- the warmth of a pomelo rind, the edge of something that bites back. One accent color, used with discipline. This is GSP's color -- inherited from jubs.studio's cyberpunk palette but made its own. Where jubs.studio uses burnt orange alongside neon green (#00FF88) in a cyberpunk clash, GSP isolates the orange and lets it burn alone against black. Clean. Singular. Unmistakable.
- **Monospace typography** -- JetBrains Mono as primary. Code is the native language; the type system says so. Inter as the complementary sans for extended reading. Shared family DNA with jubs.studio (JetBrains Mono) but GSP pairs it with Inter's warmth, not Space Grotesk's geometric edge.
- **Thick borders, flat surfaces** -- Neubrutalist DNA pushed further. No gradients, no glassmorphism, no blur. Surfaces are declared, not implied. Borders are structural, not decorative.
- **ASCII-compatible marks** -- The logo works in a terminal. It works in a commit message. It works rendered in 12px monospace. Engineering as aesthetic -- don't hide the machinery, make it beautiful (Teenage Engineering principle).
- **Avant-garde composition** -- This is not Linear with orange. GSP breaks the grid when it serves clarity, uses negative space aggressively, treats the terminal as a design medium rather than a limitation. Where jubs.studio leans cyberpunk (CRT scanlines, blinking cursors) and cyphercn leans retro-terminal, GSP is warmer and more structured -- burnt orange glow over black, clean hierarchy, systematic not atmospheric.

### 2. Personality

If GSP walked into a room.

- **Precise** -- The old craftsman. Every output is deliberate. Spacing is intentional. Token counts are considered. GSP measures twice. Precision is felt in the work, never announced in the marketing. Think: Teenage Engineering's product tolerances, Vercel's type system.
- **Irreverent** -- The child who doesn't know categories exist. The brand is literally called "Get Shit Pretty." It puts brand strategy in a terminal. It calls its pipeline a "dual diamond" without irony. It takes the craft seriously without taking itself seriously.
- **Unexpected** -- The surprise. The output always exceeds what you thought a CLI tool could do. "Wait, it did THAT?" is the consistent reaction. A design engineering system that delivers above the ceiling of expectations. The gap between input effort and output quality is the brand's signature trick.

**Embodied as a person:** A Teenage Engineering product designer who moonlights as a type foundry director. Wears all black. Laughs easily. Ships obsessively. Would rather show you the work than explain it.

### 3. Culture

The beliefs that drive every decision inside GSP.

- **Craft over speed** -- Vibe coding ships fast and breaks brands. GSP ships deliberately and builds brands that last. The journey matters. Shortcuts are debt with interest.
- **Coherence over novelty** -- A consistent brand system beats a collection of clever one-offs. The boring decision that maintains coherence wins over the exciting decision that fragments identity.
- **Design as journey, not deliverable** -- There is no "hand-off." The brand evolves through use, feedback, and iteration. GSP is a guide for the journey, not a vendor for the deliverable.
- **Constraints as creative fuel** -- Teenage Engineering builds synthesizers in a calculator form factor. GSP builds brand systems inside a terminal. The constraint is the creative advantage, not a limitation to apologize for.
- **Builder empowerment** -- GSP is open source. It orchestrates the ecosystem (Figma, Storybook, component libraries -- including cyphercn) rather than replacing it. Builders own their output. The tool serves the maker, not the other way around.
- **Community as multiplier** -- Inspired by Figma's growth engine. The best design systems are shared, forked, evolved by the community. Lone genius is a myth; collaborative craft is the reality.

### 4. Relationship

How GSP relates to the people who use it.

**The design mentor who walks with you.**

Not a tool you use and discard. Not a service that delivers and disappears. GSP is the opinionated mentor who sits beside you, pushes you to think harder about your brand decisions, and celebrates when you ship something coherent.

- **Warmth + precision** -- Anthropic's warmth in communication meets Vercel's precision in output. GSP cares about you AND holds you to a standard.
- **Guided, not automated** -- The pipeline has phases for a reason. GSP doesn't skip steps. It makes you do the brief before the build. It challenges weak positioning. It validates before it generates.
- **Pushes with love** -- Like the best mentor, GSP tells you when your color palette is incoherent or your brand voice is generic. The feedback is direct because the craft demands it.

### 5. Reflection

The idealized user that GSP portrays. Not the demographic -- the aspiration.

**The builder who ships products that look as good as they work.**

- Someone who cares about the whole thing -- not just whether it runs, but whether it holds together as a product.
- Not defined by job title. Not "developer" or "designer." A builder. Someone who makes things and wants those things to be beautiful AND functional.
- The person at the intersection: technical enough to live in the terminal, design-literate enough to care about brand coherence. Marco's side project that looks real. Suki's first shipped component. Ren's validated hybrid identity.
- Ships work that makes people ask "who designed this?" -- and the answer is "I did, with GSP."

### 6. Self-Image

How users see themselves when they use GSP.

**"I'm a builder, not just a developer. Not just a designer. I ship craft, not just code."**

- The developer who uses GSP stops saying "I'm not a designer" and starts saying "I'm a design engineer."
- The designer who uses GSP stops handing off and starts building.
- Both feel the same thing: "I make products, and my products have a brand that lasts."
- There is quiet confidence in shipping something coherent. GSP users feel that confidence. They know the brief was real, the strategy was sound, the system is consistent. They didn't just make it look nice -- they made it hold together.

## Coherence Check

All six facets tell one story: GSP is the precise, irreverent, unexpected design mentor that transforms builders (not just developers, not just designers) into people who ship craft. The black-and-burnt-orange terminal aesthetic is the physique of a culture that values constraints as creative fuel. The relationship (mentor who walks with you) delivers the self-image (I'm a builder who ships craft). Every facet reinforces the others.

## Ecosystem Differentiation

| Facet | jubs.studio | cyphercn | GSP |
|-------|-------------|----------|-----|
| Physique | Cyberpunk. Neon green + burnt orange. CRT scanlines. | Retro-terminal. Neon green. Accessible + vintage. | Citric void. Burnt orange + true black. Structured avant-garde. |
| Personality | Provocative, opinionated, loud | Utilitarian, reliable, quiet | Precise, irreverent, unexpected |
| Relationship | Founder voice -- "follow me" | Component shelf -- "take what you need" | Design mentor -- "let's build this together" |

Shared DNA across all three: dark-first, terminal-native, monospace, craft-forward, opinionated. Distinct energy: jubs.studio is the founder, cyphercn is the toolkit, GSP is the journey.

**Color inheritance:** jubs.studio's cyberpunk palette splits across its children. cyphercn inherits the neon green (#00FF88). GSP inherits the burnt orange (#FF6B35). Each sibling takes one strand of the founder's DNA and makes it singular.
