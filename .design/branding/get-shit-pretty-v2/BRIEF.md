# Brand Brief

## Brand
- **Name:** get-shit-pretty
- **Date:** 2026-03-18

## Company
- **Company name:** Get Shit Pretty (GSP)
- **Industry:** Developer tools / Design engineering
- **Founded:** 2025
- **Size:** Solo creator (jubscodes)
- **Stage:** growing
- **Existing brand?** No — starting from zero

## Brand Mode
- **Mode:** new
- **Reason:** First brand identity for an established open-source CLI tool

## Business
- **Problem:** AI makes building fast, but brand coherence falls apart at speed. Developers ship features, not products. Designers hand off, disconnected from the terminal where products are now built.
- **Solution:** A design engineering system that lives in the terminal — guides builders through the full brand-to-build journey with specialized agents. Not a tool that gives you pieces, but a system that walks you through the process.
- **Business model:** Open source CLI tool, npm package. Community-driven adoption across AI coding tool ecosystems.
- **Defensibility:** Category creation (design engineering for CLI), dual-diamond methodology baked in, multi-runtime support (Claude Code, OpenCode, Gemini CLI, Codex), network effects from community adoption.

## Personas

### Primary: Dev (The Builder Who Ships Code, Not Product)
- **Role:** Full-stack developer / indie hacker
- **Age range:** 25-40
- **Day-in-the-life:** Ships features fast with AI coding tools. Terminal is home. Knows their codebase inside out but design decisions are gut calls. Has a "good enough" landing page and inconsistent UI. Wishes the product looked as polished as the architecture.
- **Frustration:** Design feels like a separate discipline they can't access from the terminal. Hiring a designer is expensive. Design tools feel foreign. Every AI-generated UI looks the same.
- **Aspiration:** Ship products that look as good as they work. Have a coherent brand without leaving the terminal. Be proud of the whole product, not just the code.
- **Discovery:** GitHub trending, dev Twitter/Bluesky, AI coding tool communities, HN, word of mouth from other builders.
- **Trust signals:** Quality of the tool's own design (dogfooding). Open source. Terminal-native (not a web app pretending). Opinionated but not prescriptive.

### Secondary: Des (The Designer Disconnected From the New Era)
- **Role:** Product designer / brand designer
- **Age range:** 28-42
- **Day-in-the-life:** Lives in Figma, thinks in systems. Watches developers ship entire products with AI while their handoffs gather dust. Curious about the terminal but doesn't know where to start. Sees design engineering as the future but doesn't have a bridge.
- **Frustration:** Handoff culture is dying but nothing replaces it. AI coding tools don't speak design language. Component libraries give pieces, not process.
- **Aspiration:** Connect design thinking to where products are actually built now. Stay relevant as the industry shifts. Build, not just design.
- **Discovery:** Design Twitter/Bluesky, Figma community, design engineering blogs, conference talks, portfolio sites of people doing interesting work.
- **Trust signals:** Design quality of the tool itself. Understanding of real design process (not just "make it pretty"). Craft in the details.

## Brand Essence
- **Personality:** CLI-first, curious, creative
- **Personality reference:** "Like Teenage Engineering meets Vercel — a child's imagination with a craftsman's precision, wrapped in terminal confidence"
- **Emotional outcome:** Like having a talented design engineer friend who lives in your terminal — someone who geeks out about spacing and color but also understands your codebase. Approachable expertise.
- **Never be:** Corporate, generic SaaS, agency-polished, cutesy, "we hired a branding agency"
- **Tone:** Confident, irreverent, direct. Swears at the desk but ships pixel-perfect. No BS, no jargon walls. Speaks developer, thinks designer.

## Competitive Landscape
- **Direct competitors:** No direct competitor — GSP occupies a new niche at the intersection of AI-assisted development and structured design process. Adjacent tools: shadcn/ui (component library), Figma (design tool), Storybook (component docs).
- **What sets you apart?** GSP is a journey, not a tool. It guides through the full brand-to-build process inside the terminal. Others give you pieces; GSP gives you the system and walks you through it.
- **Brands admired:** Teenage Engineering (limitation as liberation, design as interface, playfulness as professionalism), Vercel (monochrome confidence, type system as identity), Anthropic (warmth meets technical craft), Figma (expanding audience without diluting), Omarchy (terminal-native beauty as philosophy, ASCII art as genuine brand identity)

## Inspiration
- **Styles liked:** Opinionated minimalism with personality. CLI aesthetic elevated to design quality. Dark mode native. Monospace as a design choice, not a limitation.
- **Styles to avoid:** Corporate, generic SaaS, agency-polished, cutesy. Anything that feels like "we hired a branding agency." Should feel built by someone who ships.
- **Existing assets:** None — starting from zero

## Constraints
- **Timeline:** No hard deadline — quality over speed
- **Budget:** Open source / bootstrapped — no paid asset licensing
- **Must-haves:** Works in terminal context (ASCII-friendly logo mark), dark mode native, strong in monochrome (color is additive, not required)
- **Non-negotiables:** The name stays. The irreverence stays. Must feel CLI-native.

## Goals
- **Business goal:** Establish GSP as the design engineering system for CLI builders. Grow adoption across Claude Code, OpenCode, Gemini, and Codex ecosystems.
- **Brand goal:** Create a recognizable identity that signals craft, confidence, and builder culture. The brand itself should be proof that GSP works.
- **Success metrics:** Brand recognition in the AI coding tool community. The identity becomes a reference for "dev tool that cares about design." Contributors and users feel proud to use it.

## Notes
- GSP is meta — it's a design tool designing itself. The brand should be living proof of the system's quality.
- Key insight: AI makes building fast, so brand coherence becomes the real challenge. GSP is the through-line.
- Dual-audience strategy: developers gain product agents, designers gain a dev tool that speaks their language. Both converge at design engineering.
- "Builders" is the shared identity — people who craft, who delve into the depths of problems to reorganize systems and bring products to being.
