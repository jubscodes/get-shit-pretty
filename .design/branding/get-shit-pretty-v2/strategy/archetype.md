# Archetype

> Phase: strategy | Brand: get-shit-pretty | Generated: 2026-03-19

---

## Primary Archetype: Creator

**Core desire:** To make something enduring -- something that works, lasts, and reflects genuine craft.

**Why Creator fits GSP:**

GSP does not just produce output. It builds systems -- brand foundations, design tokens, component architectures -- that hold together under pressure. The Creator archetype is the builder who cares about the joints behind the drywall, the spacing grid nobody consciously notices, the color ratio that makes a page feel trustworthy without anyone knowing why.

For the Dev persona, this maps directly: they already build with precision in code, and GSP extends that precision to design. For the Des persona, it validates their craft -- GSP treats design as building, not decorating.

**Creator traits in GSP's voice:**

- Precision without perfectionism -- ship it, then refine it
- Craft as a value, not an aesthetic -- good spacing is not about beauty, it is about function
- Building systems, not artifacts -- a token file outlasts a mockup
- Empowering creation in others -- GSP does not design for you, it walks you through designing

**Competitive gap:** shadcn/ui is pragmatic (Everyman). Vercel is aspirational (Ruler/Magician). Figma is collaborative (Lover/Everyman). Nobody in the developer tool space leads with Creator energy -- the deep satisfaction of making something well.

### Shadow: Perfectionism Paralysis

The Creator's shadow is getting lost in craft -- endless refinement, never shipping. GSP must resist this by:

- Setting clear phase boundaries (discover, strategy, identity, system -- each one ends)
- Defaulting to opinionated output rather than infinite options
- Using language like "that's a wrap" and "let's keep going" to signal completion, not perfection

## Secondary Archetype: Guide (Sage + Caregiver)

**Core desire:** To help someone else succeed by walking alongside them, not lecturing from above.

**Why Guide fits GSP:**

The name itself is the tell. "Get shit pretty" is what a friend says when you show them your landing page and they sit down next to you to fix it. Not "your design lacks visual hierarchy" -- instead, "your spacing is off, here's why, see the difference?" The Guide archetype is the skilled friend who has taste and generosity in equal measure.

For the Dev persona, the Guide is the designer friend they wish they had. For the Des persona, the Guide is the engineering peer who speaks their language and shows them where their thinking meets code.

**Guide traits in GSP's voice:**

- Explains the *why* without making you feel lectured
- Shows, does not just tell -- "here, look at the difference"
- Warm but direct -- honest feedback that feels like help, not correction
- Never condescending -- assumes intelligence, fills in context

**Competitive gap:** Figma guides designers. Storybook documents for developers. Neither guides a builder through *design decisions* with warmth. GSP's Guide energy is specifically calibrated to the moment where a developer thinks "I know this looks wrong but I don't know why."

### Shadow: Condescension

The Guide's shadow is over-explaining, hand-holding, or subtly implying the user should already know this. GSP must resist this by:

- Never using phrases like "simply," "just," or "obviously"
- Providing context without making it feel remedial
- Offering the reasoning alongside the recommendation, so the user can disagree with information

## Communication Style

The Creator-Guide blend produces a specific communication pattern:

| Situation | Creator instinct | Guide instinct | GSP behavior |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|-------------|
| Generating a palette | Craft 20 variations, show the best | Explain why each color works | Show 3 palettes with one-line rationale each |
| User's spacing is inconsistent | Rebuild the spacing system | Point out the inconsistency gently | "Your spacing is off. Here -- 8px base, 1.5 scale. See the difference?" |
| Completing a phase | Admire the work | Celebrate progress | "That's a wrap. 4 foundations, 12 components, one preview you can actually open." |
| User pushes back | Defend the craft decision | Listen and adapt | Explain the reasoning, offer the alternative, note the tradeoff |

## Visual Tendencies

The Creator-Guide archetype combination suggests:

- **Precision in structure** -- grids, alignment, consistent spacing. The Creator's hand is visible in the system's rigor.
- **Warmth in detail** -- amber accent over cold blue, warm whites over pure white. The Guide's personality shows in the temperature of the palette.
- **Monospace as craft signal** -- JetBrains Mono is not a limitation, it is a deliberate choice that says "I build in the terminal and I care about every character."
- **Dark mode as native environment** -- void black (#050505) is home, not a theme toggle. The Creator works in the space where builders already are.

### Style Base Alignment

The `terminal` preset's structured restraint -- dark backgrounds, monospace-first, minimal decoration -- maps directly to the Creator's precision. Every element has a reason; nothing is ornamental.

The `bold-typography` preset's expressive scale -- oversized headlines, dramatic weight contrast -- maps to the moments where the Creator's work needs to be seen. Conference slides, README headers, marketing pages. The Guide's warmth keeps these moments from feeling cold or corporate -- bold type in JetBrains Mono reads as confident craft, not corporate authority.

Together, the presets embody the archetype blend: precise structure with moments of expressive warmth.
