## Positioning Statement

For builders who ship from the terminal but want their products to earn trust, guide the eye, and communicate clearly, **GSP** is the **design process** that puts a professional design engineer on your team — walking you through brand-to-build inside your CLI, because coherent design shouldn't require a different tool or a different hire.

## Positioning Map

- **X-axis: Pieces (output/components) vs. Process (guided journey)** -- Does the tool hand you parts, or walk you through decisions?

## White Space Claim

GSP claims the upper-right quadrant -- **process-driven, developer-native** -- a position no adjacent tool occupies.

Three specific gaps GSP fills:

### 1. Design process in the terminal

Figma owns process but lives in the GUI. shadcn/ui lives in the terminal but gives pieces. Nobody walks a builder through brand decisions, color rationale, type scale choices, and component coherence from inside the CLI. GSP is the only tool where a developer types a command and gets walked through the *why* of design, not just the *what*.

### 2. Monospace-first brand identity

Vercel leads with Geist Sans. Linear leads with Inter. shadcn defaults to Inter. Every major developer tool uses a proportional typeface as its brand voice and reserves monospace for code. GSP leads with monospace -- JetBrains Mono is the primary typeface, not a code-block fallback. This signals terminal-native credibility in a way no adjacent brand does.

### 3. Warmth as a design tool value

Developer tools default to two tones: corporate-clean (Vercel, Linear) or friendly-approachable (Figma, Storybook). Neither feels like a skilled friend walking you through a decision. GSP's warmth -- casual, clear, lighthearted -- is a brand value nobody in this space has claimed. It is the tone of someone who cares about your spacing being right and will show you why 8px base with 1.5 scale makes the difference.

## Strategic Implications

- **Category language:** "Design process" as category, with "like a design engineer joined your team" as the emotional frame. Not "design engineering system" (too jargon-heavy) or "design tool" (suggests GUI). Process implies rigor and methodology; the design engineer frame implies professional-grade skill, not just assistance.
- **Competitive framing:** GSP is upstream of component libraries and downstream of design tools. It does not replace Figma or compete with shadcn -- it fills the gap between them, inside the terminal.
- **Proof obligation:** Because GSP occupies a new position, the brand itself must be the proof. The identity must demonstrate that terminal-native design guidance produces coherent, professional results. GSP designs itself -- that is the trust signal.
